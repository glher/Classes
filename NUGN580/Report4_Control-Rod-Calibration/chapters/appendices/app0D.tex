\chapter{Coefficient of determination $R^2$}
\label{app:app04}

\initial{T}his appendix presents the mathematics behind the confidence factor, also known as the $R^2$ value. The $R^2$ value, representing the fit quality, can be found using the mean ($\bar{y}$), the total sum of squares ($SS_{tot}$), and the residual sum of squares ($SS_{res}$).

Each is defined as:

\begin{equation}\label{eqd1}
\bar{y} = \frac{1}{n}\sum\limits_{i=1}^{n}{y_i}
\end{equation}

\begin{equation}\label{eqd2}
SS_{tot} = \sum_{i}{(y_i-\bar{y})^2}
\end{equation}

\begin{equation}\label{eqd3}
SS_{res} = \sum_{i}{(y_i-f_i)^2}
\end{equation}

\begin{equation}\label{eqd4}
R^2 = 1 - \frac{SS_{res}}{SS_{tot}}
\end{equation}

where:

\begin{conditions}
 f_i  &  Fitting function value at point $x_i$
\end{conditions}
